1. My dashboard answers all three questions with the three different graphs. The first lollipop chart looks at the "Top 10 Video Games of all time" by showing the Global Sales for the specific video games. As we're mainly concerned with the names of the Video Games, and not the number, the lollipop chart presents this information, while also showing the general trend of the data. The second graph is a stacked bar chart that represents sales for different regions for each video game genre. It answers the question of the most popular genre, as the overall height of the bar chart represents this. The size of each stack within a bar makes it easy to visualise the most popular region for each genre, and the color of the stack makes it easy to understand the most popular genres for each region. The tooltips provide exact numerical data related to this, as well as specify the region. The third chart answers the question of which publisher to pick based on the genre. The user can select the genre by clicking on the relevant button, then look at the first bar to get the name of the top publisher. The user can also compare how much better the publisher is for the genre comapared to the other publishers by looking the the values for Global Sales.

4. Some aspects of my dashboard that make it accessible are the tooltips for the 'Genre Sales based on Region' chart. Poeple who are pressed on time, or just want to get the overall summary of the findings can visually see the difference in the size of the bars, but if they want specific numerical data, they can hover over the block and access it easily via the tooltip. All the graphs on my dashboard have a title, and label the axis, which makes it quicker to read. When looking at the top publishers per genre, the title of the graph changes when a genre button is clicked, which makes it easily understandable, and the white space between the bars makes the data easy to read, even for people with visual impairments as it increases the contrast. The buttons are easy to click, which is helpful for users needing assistive technology. Some aspects of my dashbaord aren't accessible to users with visual impairments, especially complete blindness, as there isn't a way to access to descriptive alt text. Furthermore, users with Achromatopsia wouldn't be able to derive meaningful conclusions from the stacked bar chart without being able to discern the separate stacks that represent each region.
